## IDEO's Design Thinking (5 Phases)

1. **Empathize** — Understand users through observation, interviews, immersion
2. **Define** — Synthesize into a clear problem statement (Point of View)
3. **Ideate** — Generate wide range of solutions. Quantity over quality.
4. **Prototype** — Build low-fidelity representations to learn, not to ship
5. **Test** — Put prototypes in front of real users. Iterate.

Non-linear and iterative. Testing often loops back to empathize or define.

## Double Diamond (British Design Council)

Two diamonds: alternating divergent and convergent thinking.

**Diamond 1 — Problem Space:**
- **Discover** (Diverge): Explore broadly. Research, observation, stakeholder interviews.
- **Define** (Converge): Synthesize into a clear problem definition.

**Diamond 2 — Solution Space:**
- **Develop** (Diverge): Generate multiple solutions. Co-design, prototype.
- **Deliver** (Converge): Test, refine, implement.

Core principle: Separate idea generation from idea selection. Explore broadly before narrowing, in both problem and solution spaces.

## Google Design Sprint (Jake Knapp)

Five-day process for answering critical business questions through design, prototyping, and testing.

| Day | Focus | Key Activity |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| **Monday** | Understand | Set long-term goal, map the challenge, pick a target |
| **Tuesday** | Diverge | Individual sketching (no group brainstorming) |
| **Wednesday** | Decide | Silent critique, dot voting, storyboard the winner |
| **Thursday** | Prototype | Build a realistic-looking facade in one day |
| **Friday** | Validate | Interview 5 customers watching them use the prototype |

Key differentiator: **No group brainstorming** — individuals sketch alone to avoid groupthink. Prototype is disposable. Learn in 5 days what normally takes months.

## Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD)

People don't buy products — they hire them to do specific jobs.

**Three dimensions:**
- **Functional** — The practical task ("I need to drill a hole")
- **Emotional** — How the user wants to feel ("I want to feel accomplished")
- **Social** — How they want to be perceived ("I want my home to look nice")

**Job statement format:**
> "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]."

Teams using JTBD have achieved an 86% innovation success rate (Strategyn).

## Outcome-Driven Design

**Impact → Outcome → Output**

- **Impact**: The business result (reduce support costs)
- **Outcome**: The behavior change (users resolve issues independently)
- **Output**: The feature (redesigned help center)

An output is what you create; an outcome is the problem you solve. Design for behavior change, not feature shipping.

## Design Critique Culture

### Running Effective Critiques
- **Size**: 5-6 people maximum
- **Structure**: Designer presents context (goals, personas, constraints) before showing work
- **Feedback**: Pose as questions ("Have you considered...?") not directives ("You should...")
- **Timeboxing**: Limited time per participant forces focus on most important feedback
- **Documentation**: Notes during critique, shared afterward

### Formats
- **Round-robin**: Each participant takes turns
- **Silent critique**: Written feedback before discussion (reduces groupthink)
- **Gallery walk**: Designs posted, participants move through and annotate
